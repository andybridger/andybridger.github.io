I am an economist with more than ten years' experience in impact evaluation, econometric modelling and macroeconomic analysis, across government, industry and financial regulation. My focus is causal inference and quasi-experimental design. I take large, messy datasets and turn them into clear evidence that decision-makers can act on.

Currently Lead Economist at the Financial Conduct Authority, London.

Working Papers & Research

Estimating Tariff Equivalents of Service Trade Restrictions

MSc dissertation, commissioned by the Scottish Government · University of Edinburgh

Provides new ad-valorem tariff equivalents (AVEs) of trade restrictions in seven service sectors for 50 countries using modern structural gravity models.

SSRN paper Paper (PDF) Presentation

On International Uncertainty Links: BART-based Empirical Evidence for Australia

Bayesian econometrics project · University of Edinburgh

Uses Bayesian Additive Regression Trees (BART) to study how international uncertainty shocks pass through to the Australian economy, and compares the results against standard linear models.

Paper (PDF) GitHub repository

Australian Manufacturing in 2019

Australian Industry Group · Economic outlook · 2019

A review of Australian manufacturing, covering output, employment and operating conditions, along with the outlook for the sector.

Report (PDF)

Eastern Australian Energy Prices

Australian Industry Group · Report · 2018

An analysis of rising electricity and gas prices across eastern Australia, looking at their causes, supply-side constraints and what they meant for industry and energy policy.
